# Bicultural Integration Exchange Wiki — Claude Code Context

This is the **compounding knowledge base** for the Bicultural Integration Exchange system. It bridges the operations of mainstream commercial industries (compliance manuals, safety SOPs, performance metrics) with First Nations storytelling, scheduling, and protocol governance models.

---

## Purpose and Scope

The wiki compiles, structures, and cross-references strategic analyses, community engagement logs, pedagogical storytelling translations, and platform architectures. 

It is designed to serve as a **sovereign, local-first knowledge repository** that can be clean-transferred to an Indigenous-majority-owned firm without mixing commercial code or intellectual property constraints from other projects.

---

## Core Principles

1. **OCAP Principles Enforced**: All cultural protocols, storytelling assets, and community data must respect OCAP (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession):
   * **Ownership**: The community owns the cultural data collectively.
   * **Control**: First Nations entities dictate how information is classified and who accesses it.
   * **Access**: Members retain absolute rights of access to their registry data.
   * **Possession**: The physical database state or Markdown files must reside locally or on sovereign community servers, protected by localized decryption keys.
2. **Pedagogy through Narrative**: Respect the storytelling register as a primary vehicle for legal and operational norms, keeping it paired with — rather than subverted by — expository compliance SOPs.
3. **No Cultural Appropriation**: Storytelling patterns must draw on form, not appropriate specific sacred or owned traditional narratives. Authentic cultural stories must be contributed and managed exclusively by designated Knowledge Keepers.

## Key Rules for Agents

1. **Compounding Knowledge**: Never overwrite concept or source pages from scratch. Read their current contents, append or synthesize new details, and preserve existing structures.
2. **SOP Pairing Guideline**: When documenting industrial compliance requirements (e.g. AS-9100, ISO-9001), always attempt to pair the dry procedural specification with a relational narrative translation that highlights duty, relationship, and impact. Keep them distinct: the procedure is the *control specification*, the story is the *internalization layer*.
3. **Obsidian Wiki-Links**: Use Obsidian link syntax `[[folder/file_name]]` to build a dense, traversable knowledge graph.
4. **Local-First Verification**: Run structural checks to ensure all wikilinks resolve and files follow deterministic naming (lowercase, hyphens for spaces: `lower-kebab-case.md` or `lower_snake_case.md`).
