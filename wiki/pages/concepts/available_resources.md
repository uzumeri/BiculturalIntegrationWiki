# available_resources

This document outlines the resource constraints, technical team availability, leadership requirements, and funding strategies defined for the bicultural integration exchange ecosystem.

## 1. Architect Bandwidth (Mustafa Uzumeri)

*   **Availability**: Modest quantities of time are available free of charge to guide system architecture, review code designs, and align market physics frameworks [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L22-L22|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §4.2]].
*   **Constraints**: Bandwidth is strictly limited. If initiatives require significant operational or developer-level support, time will be rationed—potentially via consulting fees—though this is a last resort to be avoided if possible.
*   **Role**: Auxiliary. The architect provides conceptual designs and structural verification but does not manage day-to-day operations or project delivery.

---

## 2. Remote Software Engineering Team (Ethiopian Dev Team)

The core platforms (Cosolvent, ClientSynth) are developed and maintained by a remote team funded out of the architect's personal resources:

*   **Team Composition**: Three software engineers based in Ethiopia.
*   **Capacity**: Part-time and evening work structured around their primary day-job commitments. Bandwidth is variable, bounded, and subject to local connectivity or professional scheduling constraints. The team is *not* instantly deployable for time-critical, live client projects.
*   **Funding & Liability**: Funded via Upwork billing at $15 USD/hour, representing a burn rate of approximately $500–$800 USD/week. Because this is funded personally by the architect, local deployment entities carry **$0 in baseline core engine liability** during the design and simulation phases.
*   **Succession**: Transition agreements ensure departing developers onboard replacements recruited from the talent pool or diaspora networks.

---

## 3. Leadership & Champion Requirements

A market exchange cannot be successfully engineered from the outside. System operations require local operational custodians:
*   **Operational CEO / Champion**: Each local initiative (e.g. precision manufacturing, classroom exchange) must have a dedicated leader, champion, or CEO who steps up from the affected stakeholders.
*   **Governance role**: The architect will *not* assume this role. Core governance and platform access keys must reside with the community and local stakeholder champions.

---

## 4. Phased Funding Model

Initiatives are structured in two distinct investment phases to mitigate capital risk:

### 4.1 Phase 1: Conceptual Validation & Design
*   **Operational model**: A lot of high-quality work can be done by one or two people working for free, for a project grade (e.g., student co-ops, research interns), or via academic grants.
*   **Infrastructure**: Access to a capable AI coding platform (e.g., Antigravity, OpenRouter endpoints) and active outreach to compile stakeholder readiness needs.

### 4.2 Phase 2: Production System Deployment
*   **Operational model**: Standing up a production platform requires substantial funding depending on the design scope (hosting, custom database partitions, and support staff).
*   **Sourcing**: Funded through non-dilutive impact envelopes and co-op grants:
    *   *Research Co-ops*: Mitacs Accelerate, SSHRC Partnership Development envelopes [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L23-L23|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §5]].
    *   *Capital Lenders*: National Aboriginal Capital Corporations Association (NACCA), Business Development Bank of Canada (BDC) Indigenous programs.
