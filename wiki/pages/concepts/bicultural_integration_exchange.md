# bicultural_integration_exchange

The **Bicultural Integration Exchange** is a specialized matching and onboarding system designed to connect Indigenous workers with willing companies, resolving operational and cultural integration gaps via structured, protocol-guided software interventions.

## 1. System Interventions (Bicultural Stack)

The system deploys four core AI interventions to address the frictions of industrial B2B placement:

### 1.1 Dual-Register SOP Translation (Input Translation)
*   **The Problem**: Companies operate under strict, expository SOPs (e.g. ISO-9001/AS-9100) that instruct *how* and *what* to do, but fail to motivate the tirelessness required in critical processes.
*   **The Mechanism**: The platform uses **AI Input Translation** (modeled in [[wiki/pages/concepts/ai_intervention_capabilities|Capabilities]]) to pair corporate SOPs with a relational narrative register. The narrative translates dry compliance counts into relationships of stewardship, reciprocity, and duty to the end-user (see the aerospace tool control case study in [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling|Storytelling Traditions]]).
*   **Application**: The compliance manual acts as the specification; the story acts as the internalization driver.

### 1.2 Double-Blind Bicultural Content Match Stories (Trusted Intermediary)
*   **The Problem**: Candidates' narrative descriptions of experience and companies' cultural flexibility are opaque to each other, creating mutual hesitation.
*   **The Mechanism**: The system compiles a progressive **Content Match Story** that translates candidate project/community management histories into business-relevant capabilities (e.g., resource coordination, risk management) and explains corporate safety/scheduling structures in a relational context, without exposing identity or strategic constraints early in the match.

### 1.3 Shared Facilitator Pooling (Aggregation & Shadow Capacity)
*   **The Problem**: SMEs cannot afford dedicated Indigenous HR liaisons, causing onboarding failures.
*   **The Mechanism**: The system aggregates community-based Elders, HR specialists, and mentors into a shared capacity pool (see [[wiki/pages/concepts/shadow_capacity|Shadow Capacity]]). When a worker is matched, they are paired with a **Sovereign Facilitator** who mediates onboarding checkpoints, reducing the company's administrative and cognitive bandwidth overhead.

### 1.4 Dynamic Sourcing Rotation (Temporal Decoupling)
*   **The Problem**: Rigid 9-to-5 schedules conflict with First Nations community or harvest commitments, leading to high turnover.
*   **The Mechanism**: The platform models job schedules as shareable slots. It pools candidate groups and uses peer-cohort scheduling to rotate active workers, ensuring the company maintains full shift coverage while allowing workers to satisfy community and cultural obligations.

## 2. Governance: OCAP & Ostrom Commons

To avoid the Aggregator Trap (where a centralized platform extracts high margins or exploits information monopolies, see [[wiki/pages/concepts/platform_governance_models|Platform Governance]]), the exchange utilizes:
1.  **Physical Sovereignty (OCAP)**: Candidate profiles and cultural registries are hosted on local-first, community-owned servers or protected by cryptographic keys held strictly by designated Treaty 7 custodians.
2.  **Ostrom Principles**: Standard platform parameters (minimum wages, scheduling flexibility thresholds, protocol conformance) are managed via Elinor Ostrom's design principles, establishing active local monitoring and community voice over the matching engine.
