# bicultural_integration_exchange

The **Bicultural Integration Exchange** is a specialized matching and onboarding system designed to connect Indigenous workers with willing companies, resolving operational and cultural integration gaps via structured, protocol-guided software interventions [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L11-L16|Source Bicultural Integration Exchange System, §1]]. The system is commercialized and deployed by the [[wiki/pages/entities/storyboard_integration_systems|StoryBoard Integration Systems]] startup platform [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L27-L30|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §1]].

## 1. System Interventions (Bicultural Stack)

The system deploys four core AI interventions to address the frictions of industrial B2B placement:

### 1.1 Dual-Register SOP Translation (Input Translation)
*   **The Problem**: Companies operate under strict, expository SOPs (e.g. ISO-9001/AS-9100) that instruct *how* and *what* to do, but fail to motivate the tirelessness required in critical processes.
*   **The Mechanism**: The platform uses **AI Input Translation** (modeled in [[wiki/pages/concepts/ai_intervention_capabilities|Capabilities]]) to pair corporate SOPs with a relational narrative register, compiling them into a [[wiki/pages/concepts/dual_register_playbook|Dual-Register Playbook]] [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L27-L30|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §1]]. The narrative translates dry compliance counts into relationships of stewardship, reciprocity, and duty to the end-user (see the aerospace tool control case study in [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling|Storytelling Traditions]]).
*   **Application**: The compliance manual acts as the specification; the story acts as the internalization driver [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L21-L21|Source Bicultural Integration Exchange System, §2.1]].

### 1.2 Double-Blind Bicultural Content Match Stories (Trusted Intermediary)
*   **The Problem**: Candidates' narrative descriptions of experience and companies' cultural flexibility are opaque to each other, creating mutual hesitation.
*   **The Mechanism**: The system compiles a progressive **Content Match Story** that translates candidate project/community management histories into business-relevant capabilities (e.g., resource coordination, risk management) and explains corporate safety/scheduling structures in a relational context, without exposing identity or strategic constraints early in the match [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L22-L22|Source Bicultural Integration Exchange System, §2.2]].

### 1.3 Shared Facilitator Pooling (Aggregation & Shadow Capacity)
*   **The Problem**: SMEs cannot afford dedicated Indigenous HR liaisons, causing onboarding failures.
*   **The Mechanism**: The system aggregates community-based Elders, HR specialists, and mentors into a shared capacity pool (see [[wiki/pages/concepts/shadow_capacity|Shadow Capacity]]). When a worker is matched, they are paired with a **Sovereign Facilitator** who mediates onboarding checkpoints, reducing the company's administrative and cognitive bandwidth overhead [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L23-L23|Source Bicultural Integration Exchange System, §2.3]].

### 1.4 Dynamic Sourcing Rotation (Temporal Decoupling)
*   **The Problem**: Rigid 9-to-5 schedules conflict with First Nations community or harvest commitments, leading to high turnover.
*   **The Mechanism**: The platform models job schedules as shareable slots. It pools candidate groups and uses peer-cohort scheduling to rotate active workers, ensuring the company maintains full shift coverage while allowing workers to satisfy community and cultural obligations [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L24-L24|Source Bicultural Integration Exchange System, §2.4]].

## 2. Governance: OCAP & Ostrom Commons

To avoid the Aggregator Trap (where a centralized platform extracts high margins or exploits information monopolies, see [[wiki/pages/concepts/platform_governance_models|Platform Governance]]), the exchange utilizes:
1.  **Physical Sovereignty (OCAP)**: Candidate profiles and cultural registries are hosted on local-first, community-owned servers or protected by cryptographic keys held strictly by designated Treaty 7 custodians.
2.  **Ostrom Principles**: Standard platform parameters (minimum wages, scheduling flexibility thresholds, protocol conformance) are managed via Elinor Ostrom's design principles, establishing active local monitoring and community voice over the matching engine [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L25-L25|Source Bicultural Integration Exchange System, §2.5]].
