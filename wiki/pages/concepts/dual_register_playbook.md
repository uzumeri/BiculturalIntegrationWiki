# dual_register_playbook

A **Dual-Register Playbook** is a process documentation standard that pairs a standard operating procedure (SOP) with a relational storytelling register, allowing compliance rules to remain rigid while changing the motivational register through narrative pedagogy [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L27-L30|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §1]].

## 1. Expository vs. Narrative Registers

The playbook segregates standard documentation into two parallel tracks [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L39-L49|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §2]]:

### 1.1 Expository Register (The Audit Baseline)
*   **Form**: Expository, abstract, passive voice, rules-first, and atemporal.
*   **Focus**: Instructs *what* to do and *how* to measure it. Represents the formal standard required for regulatory audits (such as the AS9100 or ISO-9001 aerospace standards detailed in [[wiki/pages/concepts/mfg_requirements|mfg_requirements]]) [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L15-L16|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §1.1]].
*   **Failure Mode**: High cognitive load and alienation when used as the sole learning container, telling the worker the rules but failing to drive internal compliance under fatigue or pressure [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L17-L19|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §1.1]].

### 1.2 Narrative Register (The Motivational Interface)
*   **Form**: Storytelling, consequence-based, relational, active voice, and temporal.
*   **Focus**: Tells a story about the task, illustrating the relationship of reciprocity and consequences.
*   **Mechanism**: The narrative register **never states the rule directly**. Instead, it places the task in relation to other people or the environment and withholds explicit moral declarations, requiring the listener to turn the narrative over internally to extract the compliance norm [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L56-L60|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §2.1]].

## 2. Linguistic and Pedagogical Grounding

The Dual-Register Playbook is grounded in North American Indigenous language structures and traditional pedagogical methods:

### 2.1 Relational vs. Expository Causation
Expository SOPs assume a mechanical causation model ("do X to prevent Y"). In contrast, verb-centered grammars (such as Algonquian languages like Cree or Ojibwe) grammaticalize cause-and-effect as a relational event (e.g. through causative suffixes or moods) [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L19-L20|Linguistic Traditions, §1]]. This structures causation relational to system/environment ("under what circumstances X occurs" or "how one stands in relation to Y") rather than dry mechanical assertions [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L20-L20|Linguistic Traditions, §1]].

### 2.2 Storytelling as a Normative Vessel
In Indigenous oral traditions, stories are not merely decorative framing; they are the primary legal and pedagogical container of norms, rights, and responsibilities [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L22-L23|Linguistic Traditions, §1]]. Using narratives to convey shop-floor constraints (like tool control or lockout-tagout) respects this normative structure, translating compliance checks into relational obligations [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L23-L23|Linguistic Traditions, §1]].

## 3. Worked Examples

### 3.1 High-Reliability Tool Control SOP
*   **Expository Instruction**: *Closed-loop tool control. Foam shadow boards must be verified at the start and end of every shift. Discrepancies must be reported to the quality inspector immediately.* [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L33-L34|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].
*   **Narrative Translation**: A mentor guides a new worker and points to an empty cutout on the foam board. He tells the story of a worker who left a single socket tool in an engine because he was rushed and trusted his memory. The tool caused an engine failure and a crash in flight. The mentor notes: *"The board is there because you don't get to fly with them. The count is the one promise you get to make to people you'll never meet."* [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L34-L35|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].

### 3.2 Common-Pool Resource Extraction SOP
*   **Expository Instruction**: *Fisheries extraction quota compliance. Weirs must not block more than 50% of the channel. Extraction windows are bounded by seasonal spawning counts.* [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L30-L30|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].
*   **Narrative Translation**: A story of two fishers. The younger fisher blocks the narrows completely with a weir to maximize his immediate catch, laughing at the empty net of the older fisher. By the third spring, the younger has no fish. The older fisher, who only blocks the narrows halfway to let the spawn escape, notes: *"I never asked the river to feed me. I fed the river."* [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L30-L32|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].
