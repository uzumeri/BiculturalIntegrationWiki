# source_manyguns_roadmap

This page serves as a citation target for the strategic roadmap *Thought Experiment: From the Indigenous Knowledge Exchange to a Sovereign Market-Engineering Firm* (§1).

## Key Claims

- **Dual Build Sequencing Strategy**: Recommends proving matching physics first via a risk-free digital twin simulation of corrections Elder matching (The Empty Lodge) before launching the first live, lower-stakes classroom matching market (The Indigenous Knowledge Exchange) at Mount Royal University (MRU) (§2).
- **Physical Sovereignty as a Database Standard**: Dictates separating the open-source matching engine (Cosolvent) from the database state, hosting candidate and Elder registries on secure community-owned local nodes (enforcing OCAP) rather than centralized commercial clouds (§1.3).
- **Capital-Efficient Pacing & AI-Augmented Building**: Recommends a personal-contribution funding pace ($800/week) and leveraging AI-augmented coding (Antigravity) to build strategic assets in parallel, matching the long timelines required for institutional relationship trust-building (§1).
- **Procurement and CCIB Moat**: Bicultural joint ventures built on the MarketForge stack can secure set-aside Moats under the Canadian Procurement Strategy for Aboriginal Business (PSAB) and federal 5% Indigenous procurement targets (§1.2).
- **Service Stack requirements**: Operational scale requires dedicated service layers, including customer support, user recruiting through Treaty 7 sub-registries, and immediate honoraria bridging to bypass university reconciliation lags (§4.1).
- **Succession and Architect Role**: The architect's role is strictly auxiliary (system specification, code design reviews, no salary or core governance) with the goal of complete succession and handover of the matching keys to Indigenous owners within three years (§2).
- **Grants & Capital Structuring**: Pilots can be funded through applied-research envelopes (SSHRC Partnership Development Grants, Mitacs Accelerate) and transition to impact-lending capital (NACCA, BDC Indigenous programs, Heritage Canada) (§5).
