# source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system

This page serves as a citation target for the design document *Bicultural Integration Exchange System: A Cosolvent-Based Design Analysis* (§1).

## Key Claims

- **Onboarding Mismatch as Primary Friction**: Placement failures in B2B corporate settings (e.g. manufacturing, aerospace) are primarily driven by cultural and operational onboarding mismatches rather than simple skill or job availability. Frictions include corporate cognitive bandwidth limits and candidate profile opacity (§1).
- **Tripartite Matching Model**: Connects the Candidate (narrative profile), the Company (readiness profile), and the Community Facilitator/Mentor (relational custodian) simultaneously (§1).
- **Dual-Register SOP Translation**: Uses AI Input Translation to pair strict compliance SOPs with relational, consequence-based storytelling patterns to improve worker internalization (§2.1).
- **Double-Blind Bicultural Content Match Stories**: Compiles double-blind narratives that match candidate narrative-expressed capabilities with corporate support structures without exposing sensitive data early in the negotiation (§2.2).
- **Shared Facilitator Pooling**: Pools community-based Elders and cultural HR facilitators as a shared service, mitigating SME overhead and providing onboarding support (§2.3).
- **Dynamic Sourcing Rotation**: Rotates worker shifts dynamically in aggregated pools to allow participants to fulfill community/cultural commitments while maintaining continuous operational coverage (§2.4).
- **Ostrom-Based OCAP Platform Governance**: Enforces data sovereignty (OCAP) at the hosting layer and manages platform parameters (wages, standards, scheduling) via Elinor Ostrom's design principles to prevent operator extraction (§2.5).
- **Mitacs and ClientSynth Simulation Validation**: Suggests using ClientSynth to model digital twin matches and validation metrics prior to live funding (§3).
