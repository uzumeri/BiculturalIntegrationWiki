# source_storyboard_competitive_landscape

This page serves as a citation target for the competitive landscape report *Competitive Landscape & Market Whitespace: Bicultural Process Design in Canada* (§1).

## Key Claims

- **Attitude vs. Operational Task Restructuring**: Standard models (like San'yas Cultural Safety Training) focus exclusively on the general staff awareness level, without modifying daily workplace instructions, machine-level SOPs, or shop-floor controls (§2.1).
- **Consulting vs. Software Scalability**: Specialized strategic consulting firms (such as Len Pierre Consulting) are highly human-intensive and lack automated translation tooling, matching algorithms, or database platform extensions (§2.2).
- **Policy vs. Process Control**: HR guides and tribal council toolkits (like Nokiiwin or MiHR manuals) focus on corporate policy accommodations (funeral leave, transport) rather than real-time process execution or tool control compliance on the shop floor (§2.3).
- **Audit Criteria vs. Execution Engines**: National institutional standards (like HSO 75000:2022) establish certification criteria for auditing cultural safety but do not provide the concrete translation engines or placement engines to implement them (§2.4).
- **SOP Translation Whitespace**: No competitor currently translates standard operating procedures or pairs them with narrative motivational registers to support quality compliance (§3.1).
- **Matching Technology Inadequacy**: Existing job boards are keyword-based resume filters that force candidates to conform to Western formats and fail to assess employer readiness or run simulations (e.g. ClientSynth) (§3.2).
- **Two-Eyed Seeing as a Process Engineering System**: StoryBoard differentiates itself by translating theoretical reconciliation frameworks into a scalable software and process engineering platform, securing database-level OCAP sovereignty (§4).
