# double_blind_matching

The **Double-Blind Bicultural Matching** initiative focuses on resolving trust deficits and strategic exposure risks between First Nations candidates and corporate employers. Using a progressive disclosure protocol running on the Cosolvent engine, the platform translates narrative-expressed capabilities into equivalent industrial skill taxonomies while assessing employer readiness before revealing identities [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L20-L21|Source BCI System, §2.2]].

## 2. Inception Context

Traditional applicant tracking systems rely on linear resumes and keyword matching that penalize non-standard employment records and narrative-based capability descriptions. This mismatch results in candidate profile opacity and high cognitive bandwidth costs for hiring managers [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L14-L16|Source BCI System, §1]]. Under this initiative, the platform uses two-register matching ontology mapping to identify capabilities from community or land stewardship and pairs them with employer readiness profiles, ensuring a trust-gated mutual fit prior to disclosure [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L20-L21|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §3.1]].
