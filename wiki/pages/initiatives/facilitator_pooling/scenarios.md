# facilitator_pooling_scenarios

This document records the worked case studies and intervention scenarios used to train Shared Facilitators and evaluate platform protocols [[wiki/pages/sources/source_storyboard_competitive_landscape#L17-L19|Source Competitive Landscape, §2]].

## 1. Case Study: Resolving Early Shift Alignment Friction

### The Setup
*   **Employer**: Precision Aerospace Parts (Ontario SME).
*   **Candidate**: CANDIDATE-LOG-702 (Siksika Nation member).
*   **Shared Facilitator**: FACILITATOR-ELD-02 (Tsuut'ina Nation Elder/HR Specialist).
*   **Trigger**: The weekly token check flagged a drop in the candidate's comfort rating. The supervisor noted that the candidate had missed a Monday morning toolbox safety meeting.

### Facilitator Intervention
1.  **Immediate Check-in**: FACILITATOR-ELD-02 reached out to the candidate and discovered they were providing elder support for a family member during a local storm event, which delayed their travel back to Calgary.
2.  **Tripartite Consultation**: The facilitator met with the shop floor supervisor and candidate:
    *   Explained the candidate's family responsibility structure within a relational framework rather than framing it as a "rules violation."
    *   Supported the supervisor in explaining the high safety risks of missing the start-of-shift toolbox meeting.
3.  **Resolution**: The facilitator assisted the candidate in scheduling an alternative safety briefing, and worked with the supervisor to set up a communication protocol for future emergencies.

## 2. Case Study: Preparing SME Supervisor for Seasonal Harvest Leave

### The Setup
*   **Employer**: Boreal Precision Fab (Aero Supplier).
*   **Candidate**: CANDIDATE-MACH-405.
*   **Shared Facilitator**: FACILITATOR-ELD-02.
*   **Trigger**: The 30-day checkpoint triggered a reminder that the seasonal berry and meat harvest was approaching, requiring the candidate to take five consecutive days of personal leave.

### Facilitator Intervention
1.  **Readiness Preparation**: The facilitator held a 30-minute briefing with the Boreal shop manager.
    *   Framed the harvest leave as a deep cultural obligation that sustains community health, not as a casual vacation.
    *   Shared templates for setting up dynamic scheduling rotations.
2.  **Onsite Briefing**: The facilitator and candidate reviewed the production schedule:
    *   Identified shift slots that could be pre-loaded or covered by rotating peers.
    *   Coordinated with the candidate to document their safety checklist sign-offs prior to departure.
3.  **Resolution**: The candidate took the harvest leave with no operational impact, and returned to a stable, welcoming shop floor with no loss of reputation.
