# storyboard_playbooks_risks

This risk register outlines potential operational, cultural, and validation failure modes for the Dual-Register Playbook initiative [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L15-L19|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §1.1]].

## 1. Risk Register Matrix

| Risk ID | Description | Severity | Probability | Mitigation Protocol |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **RP-01** | **OCAP Sovereignty Breach**: Local stories or cultural metaphors are exported or indexed outside their designated Treaty 7 community databases. | Critical | Medium | **Sovereign Custody Keys**: Decryption keys reside exclusively with the First Nations custodians. Automated CommonContext audits block external replication [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L64-L68|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §2.2]]. |
| **RP-02** | **Compliance Misalignment**: The narrative register obscures or alters the technical compliance checklist, leading to ISO-9001 or AS-9100 quality audit failures. | High | Low | **Decoupled Verification Loop**: Playbooks undergo a double-signoff process. A Quality Engineer signs off on the expository track, and a Community Facilitator signs off on the narrative track [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_onboarding_prospectus#L58-L58|Source Onboarding Prospectus, §2.1]]. |
| **RP-03** | **Knowledge Keeper Overload**: The manual curation loop for bicultural translation relies on a small circle of Elders, leading to process delays and burnout. | Medium | High | **Pre-vetted Template Libraries**: AI engines use generalized bicultural story frameworks for baseline operations, requiring Elders only for vertical-specific customization [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L52-L56|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §3]]. |
| **RP-04** | **Participant Skepticism**: Workers find the storytelling register patronizing if stories are poorly calibrated or feel manufactured. | High | Medium | **Authentic Curation Protocol**: Reject generic, corporate-generated parables. Only stories sourced directly from the local oral history archives are permitted for translation. |

---

## 2. Validation Metrics

Prior to factory-floor pilot runs, each playbook is scored against three criteria:
1.  **Semantic Equivalence**: The narrative must map 1-to-1 onto the expository safety checkpoints.
2.  **Cultural Integrity**: The story must conform to the Treaty 7 pedagogical standards and contain no commercial or proprietary copyright claims on oral narratives.
3.  **Simulation Pass**: The playbook must demonstrate a turnover reduction of at least 15% in ClientSynth onboarding simulations.
