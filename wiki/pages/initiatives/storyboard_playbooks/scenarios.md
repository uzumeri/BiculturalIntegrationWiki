# storyboard_playbooks_scenarios

This document records the worked bicultural translation scenarios and case narratives used to train the AI Input Translation models [[wiki/pages/sources/source_storyboard_competitive_landscape#L84-L84|Source Competitive Landscape, §4]].

## 1. Case Study: High-Reliability Aerospace Tool Control

### The Expository Baseline
*   *SOP Code*: QA-TC-004
*   *Audit Requirement*: Closed-loop tool control. Foam shadow boards must be verified at the start and end of every shift. Discrepancies must be reported to the quality inspector immediately [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L33-L34|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].

### The Narrative Overlay
A mentor takes the new worker to the foam board and points to an empty shape. He tells the story of a worker who left one tool behind because he was in a hurry and trusted his own memory. The tool rode up in the jet engine, causing a crash. The mentor states:
> *"The board is there because you don't get to fly with them. The count is the one promise you get to make to people you'll never meet."* [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L34-L35|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].

## 2. Case Study: Common-Pool Resource Extraction

### The Expository Baseline
*   *SOP Code*: ENV-WEIR-001
*   *Audit Requirement*: Weir operations. The channel weir must not block more than 50% of the active water channel during seasonal fish spawning runs [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L30-L30|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].

### The Narrative Overlay
The playbook records the oral history of two fishers. The younger fisher blocks the narrows completely to maximize his immediate catch, laughing at the half-empty nets of the older fisher. By the third spring, the younger fisher has no catch because the spawn died out. The older fisher, who only blocks the narrows halfway, tells him:
> *"I never asked the river to feed me. I fed the river."* [[wiki/pages/sources/indigenous_languages_storytelling#L30-L32|Linguistic Traditions, §2]].

## 3. Case Study: Manufacturing Lock-Out Tag-Out (LOTO)

### The Expository Baseline
*   *SOP Code*: SAFE-LOTO-012
*   *Audit Requirement*: Lock-out tag-out. Energy isolation devices must be locked and tagged prior to servicing machinery. Only the technician placing the lock has authorization to remove it.

### The Narrative Overlay
A storyteller shares the history of the "Red Lock." A technician left his shift early, leaving his lock on the breaker. A coworker removed the lock with bolt cutters to start the conveyor. The technician, who had returned to retrieve a dropped wrench, was caught in the conveyor. The story frames the lock not as an administrative procedure, but as the owner's "life-shadow" on the wire:
> *"The lock is your hand on the main switch. When you cut another man's lock, you are cutting his lifeline."*
