# platform_commons

The **Ostrom-Based Platform Commons** initiative defines the governance structures, data sovereignty parameters, and cooperative ownership models for the bicultural integration exchange. Built on Elinor Ostrom's commons design principles and First Nations OCAP (Ownership, Control, Access, and Possession) standards, the platform operates as a community-controlled utility rather than an extractive intermediary [[wiki/pages/sources/source_bicultural_integration_exchange_system#L23-L23|Source BCI System, §2.5]].

## 2. Inception Context

Standard marketplace platforms decay into extractive monopolies ("Aggregator Traps") that monetize participant data and squeeze labor margins. For First Nations candidates and communities, this extraction represents a critical trust barrier and historical threat to data sovereignty [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L18-L18|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §1.3]]. This initiative establishes a cooperative protocol that contractually and physically separates registries onto community-controlled local nodes, enforcing local monitoring and mutual agreement on wage scales and workplace standards [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L22-L22|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §2]].
