# manyguns_knowledge_exchange

The **Indigenous Knowledge Exchange** is a protocol-governed matching service designed to connect Treaty 7 knowledge keepers, language teachers, artists, and professionals with classrooms at Mount Royal University (MRU) and regional school boards [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L22-L23|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §1]]. 

The initiative is incubated alongside a proof-of-concept simulation of corrections Elder matching (The Empty Lodge) to demonstrate sovereign market-engineering capabilities [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L21-L22|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §1]].

## 1. The Staged Roadmap

The initiative is structured across five major stages of capability ramp-up:

### 1.1 Stage 0: Conditions Precedent (Months 0–2)
Establishment of the core foundation [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L62-L72|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 0]]:
*   **Business Lead**: Recruitment of an Indigenous CEO to manage sponsors and university relationships.
*   **Federated Governance**: Stand up a Nation-specific Elders' advisory circle to guide the registry, separate from the company's corporate assets.
*   **Incubation Host**: MRU serves as the host institution and the first client, satisfying its indigenization mandate under TRC Calls to Action 62–65.
*   **Engineering Team**: Core software support provided part-time by the Ethiopian remote team under DeeperPoint.

### 1.2 Stage 1: Digital Twin & Pilot Build (Months 2–8)
Development of two parallel artifacts [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L83-L94|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 1]]:
*   **The Empty Lodge Twin**: A synthetic facility and Elder database simulation running on [[wiki/pages/concepts/bicultural_integration_exchange|ClientSynth]] to demonstrate OCAP database structures and matching logic without exposing real data.
*   **The Exchange Pilot**: A functional, pilot-ready platform for MRU classrooms, utilizing Siksika and Treaty 7 sub-registries.

### 1.3 Stage 2: Live Operations (Months 8–20)
Pilot launch at MRU to verify matching physics, load distribution across carriers, and honoraria processes [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L99-L109|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 2]]. To bypass university accounting reconciliation delays, the platform implements an **Honoraria Bridging Service** that pays knowledge keepers same-day [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L120-L120|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 2]].

### 1.4 Stage 3: Adjacent Expansion (Months 14–26)
Replicating the matching templates for adjacent local thin-market needs at declining marginal cost, such as:
*   Language keepers matched to immersion schools [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L129-L129|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 3]].
*   Certified water-treatment operators matched to remote reserves [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L130-L130|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 3]].
*   On-reserve housing-repair contracting pools [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L131-L131|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 3]].

### 1.5 Stage 4: Scale & Independence (Months 24–36)
Transitioning the live corrections matching system (The Empty Lodge) into community-managed Section 81 Healing Lodges, establishing a self-sustaining Indigenous-owned market-engineering firm [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L140-L144|Source Manyguns Roadmap, Stage 4]].

## 2. Technology & Data Sovereignty

The platform runs a specialized configuration of the open-source **MarketForge** stack:
*   **Decoupled Database Model**: Elder registrations and Treaty 7 protocol guidelines are hosted on sovereign, local community databases. The matching algorithm does not store or centralize private data, satisfying the highest standards of **OCAP** (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession) data sovereignty [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L38-L39|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §1.3]].
*   **Protocol Calendars**: The CommonContext metadata layer flags matching matches that conflict with ceremony calendars, seasonal harvests, or community obligations [[wiki/pages/concepts/bicultural_integration_exchange|CommonContext Specification]].

## 3. Funding & Capital Structuring

The pilot phases leverage non-dilutive applied-research grant envelopes before transition to commercial debt and equity:
*   **Build Phase**: SSHRC Partnership Development Grants ($75k–$200k over two years) and Mitacs Accelerate intern funding to support student developers from MRU [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L157-L157|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §5]].
*   **Venture Launch**: Capital sourced from the National Aboriginal Capital Corporations Association (NACCA) Indigenous Growth Fund and BDC Indigenous Entrepreneur loans to maintain majority Indigenous ownership [[wiki/pages/sources/source_manyguns_roadmap#L157-L157|Source Manyguns Roadmap, §5]].
